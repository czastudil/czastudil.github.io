Project Description

CampusPartner is an senior capstone project in the Department of Computer Science at JMU focused on assistive technology and pedestrian navigation around James Madison University's campus. I realized there was a need for an application like this because the campus is very hilly (there are pros and cons to being nestled in the mountains by the Shenandoah Valley), and visitors or people who have mobility impairments may not know accessible routes around campus. This is where my work comes in. I am using Open Street Maps and GraphHopper to pull map annotations and routes that abide by a user's mobility requirements and creates pedestrian routes for them to follow. This is very much a prototype, but you can see some of the work I have presented at the links below, as well as a video demonstration of the application as it is at the time of submission of my project. If you are interested in seeing the source code, the link to the GitHub repository is below.
